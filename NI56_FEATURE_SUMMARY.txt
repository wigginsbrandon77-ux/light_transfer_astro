NEW FEATURE: NICKEL-56 RADIOACTIVE DECAY HEATING
=================================================

WHAT'S NEW
----------

The radiative transfer code now includes Ni-56 radioactive decay as a 
heating source! This models the energy input that powers supernova light curves.

QUICK START
-----------

Enable Ni-56 heating with one option:

    make
    ./radtransfer output_000050.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.6 -time 20

This simulates a supernova with:
- 0.6 solar masses of Ni-56 (typical for Type Ia)
- 20 days after explosion (near peak light)

PHYSICS INCLUDED
----------------

Decay chain: Ni-56 (6.1 days) → Co-56 (77 days) → Fe-56 (stable)

Energy release:
  - Ni-56: 1.75 MeV per decay
  - Co-56: 3.61 MeV per decay

Effects:
  ✓ Heats inner ejecta where Ni-56 is located
  ✓ Increases temperature → brighter, bluer spectrum
  ✓ Time-dependent luminosity following decay rates
  ✓ Powers the supernova light curve

NEW COMMAND-LINE OPTIONS
------------------------

-ni56 <mass>
    Initial Ni-56 mass in solar masses
    Example: -ni56 0.6
    Range: 0.01-1.0 M_sun (typical)

-ni56_radius <radius>
    Radius containing Ni-56 in code units
    Default: 0.3
    Example: -ni56_radius 0.2

-time <days>
    Time since explosion in days
    Default: 10
    Example: -time 30

USAGE EXAMPLES
--------------

1. Type Ia supernova at peak:
   ./radtransfer output.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.6 -time 20 -opacity gray

2. Core-collapse supernova early:
   ./radtransfer output.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.1 -time 5

3. Time series for light curve:
   for t in 5 10 20 30 50; do
       ./radtransfer output.vtk spec_${t}d.txt -ni56 0.6 -time $t
   done

4. Compare with and without heating:
   ./radtransfer output.vtk no_ni56.txt
   ./radtransfer output.vtk with_ni56.txt -ni56 0.6 -time 20

WHAT YOU'LL SEE
---------------

With Ni-56 heating enabled:
  ✓ Higher total luminosity
  ✓ Spectrum peaks at higher frequency (bluer)
  ✓ More UV/optical flux
  ✓ Realistic supernova brightness

Without Ni-56 heating:
  ✗ Only adiabatic cooling
  ✗ Ejecta gets dimmer and redder with time
  ✗ Unrealistically faint

TYPICAL PARAMETERS
------------------

Type Ia supernovae:
  - Ni-56 mass: 0.4-0.8 M_sun (canonical: 0.6)
  - Ni-56 radius: 0.2-0.4 (inner ejecta)
  - Peak time: ~17-20 days

Core-collapse supernovae:
  - Ni-56 mass: 0.01-0.2 M_sun
  - Ni-56 radius: 0.1-0.3 (compact core)
  - Peak time: ~10-20 days

LUMINOSITY EVOLUTION
--------------------

For 0.6 M_sun of Ni-56:

Days    Luminosity (erg/s)    Dominant isotope
  1     1.2 × 10^43           Ni-56 (100%)
  5     9.8 × 10^42           Ni-56 (95%)
 10     7.7 × 10^42           Ni-56 (85%)
 20     4.8 × 10^42           Ni-56 (65%)
 50     1.5 × 10^42           Co-56 (65%)
100     3.0 × 10^41           Co-56 (85%)

The luminosity decreases with time as the Ni-56 and Co-56 decay.

COMBINING WITH OPACITY MODELS
------------------------------

Ni-56 heating works with all three opacity models:

Gray opacity (recommended for seeing peak):
    ./radtransfer output.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.6 -opacity gray

Kramers (physically motivated):
    ./radtransfer output.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.6 -opacity kramers

Free-free (for ionized ejecta):
    ./radtransfer output.vtk spectrum.txt -ni56 0.6 -opacity freefree

VALIDATION CHECKS
-----------------

1. Check luminosity is reasonable:
   L ~ 10^42 - 10^43 erg/s for 0.6 M_sun at 20 days

2. Temperature should increase:
   Expect ΔT ~ few × 10^3 K in heated region

3. Spectrum should be brighter and bluer:
   Compare spectrum with and without -ni56 flag

4. Time dependence should follow decay:
   L(20d) > L(50d) > L(100d)

OUTPUT FILE
-----------

The spectrum.txt file now includes Ni-56 information in header:

# Ni-56 DECAY HEATING:
#   Enabled:          Yes
#   Ni-56 mass:       0.600 M_sun
#   Ni-56 radius:     0.300 (code units)
#   Time since explosion: 20.00 days
#   Decay luminosity: 4.80e+42 erg/s

This makes it easy to track parameters when comparing different runs.

IMPLEMENTATION DETAILS
----------------------

The code:
1. Calculates total decay luminosity L(t) from Ni-56 and Co-56
2. Deposits energy uniformly in region r < ni56_radius
3. Converts energy deposition to temperature increase
4. Assumes full gamma-ray trapping (optically thick)
5. Updates temperature field before ray tracing

Assumptions:
- Uniform Ni-56 distribution within specified radius
- Full trapping of decay energy (no escape)
- Instantaneous thermalization
- No mixing or diffusion
